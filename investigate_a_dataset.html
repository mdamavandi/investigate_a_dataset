Introduction¶

The datasets I've chosen are from Gapminder World. The first details the hourly compensation for workers throughout the world. The second details the average working hours per week of workers around the world. The last dataset is the trade balances of these countries. Each of these datasets is broken out by country and year.

There has been a lot of talk (from the Executive Branch of the US government) about how the trade balance of the United States has negatively affected the country. I wanted to explore how it may have affected the workers of this country. I also wanted to explore which countries were performing better relative to their peers in terms of wages and working hours, and worse in terms of trade balance.

def unpivot(data, valueName):
    return data.melt(id_vars=['country'], var_name='year', value_name=valueName).dropna(subset=[valueName])

Research Question 1: Top Performers for Workers, Worst Performer for Trade¶

Which of the countries are performing better in wages and working hours? Which country is performing worse in trade balance? We'll now calculate the percent change in wages & working hours, and the rate of change of the trade balance. (I explored calculating the percent change for trade balance, but because the numbers are very large, comparatively small changes made it difficult to interpret the results.) Best cases for workers would be increased wages and lowered working hours, worst case for trade would be a higher trade deficit.

Conclusions¶

In summary, Korea, Rep. performed the best for workers over the 25 year period defined by the dataset (the wages had an average percent change of 79.00% every five years and the working hours had a percent change of -3.88% every five years. The United States performed the worst with regards to their trade balance (growing their trade deficit at an average rate of 28.4 billion dollars every five years). There doesn't appear to be any correlation between the trade balance and worker wages or hours.

One of the major limitations I came up against was that the data was only current to 2007 and many of the countries reported only sporadically. It would be great to do a similar analysis with more up-to-date data and to potentially include some sort of statistical analyses or regression models to help us better understand the traits we chose to examine.
